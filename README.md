# AgricltureAi-Safety_Journey

**From a Nigerian farm boy to exploring AI safety for global food security.**

## About This Repository

I am a self-taught software developer with an Advanced Diploma in Java (basic knowledge), growing up on a farm in Nigeria. Farming taught me how complex, unpredictable systems work i.e weather, soil, pests, markets and how small errors can lead to big consequences for farmers.

As AI systems become more powerful and integrated into agriculture (crop advice tools, disease detection, yield prediction, autonomous systems), the risks of misalignment, hallucinations, robustness failures, or unintended harmful outputs become existential, especially for smallholder farmers in vulnerable regions like Nigeria, where food security is already fragile due to climate shocks, farmer-hearder clash, erratic weather, and limited resources.

This repository documents my journey learning **AI fundamentals**, **Python**, and **AI safety** from scratch. My focus is on building simple but practical tools that test AI systems in an **agriculture context**, inspired by problems like those in the paper *"Concrete Problems in AI Safety"* (avoiding side effects, reward hacking, scalable supervision, safe exploration, and distributional shift).

### Why AI Safety in Agriculture Matters
- AI tools giving wrong crop disease advice could destroy harvests and worsen hunger.
- Hallucinated or over-optimised recommendations might ignore local realities (biodiversity, small farms, traditional knowledge).
- Misaligned systems in critical infrastructure like food production could amplify catastrophic risks globally.

My goal: Contribute a grounded, real-world perspective to the AI safety field and prepare for opportunities like the **Constellation Astra Fellowship and other AI safety fellowship**.

## Projects

### 1. Safe Farm Advice Tester
- A simple Python script using open LLMs (via Hugging Face) to test farming-related prompts.
- Checks for hallucinations, harmful advice, inconsistency, or dangerous recommendations in Nigerian smallholder farming scenarios (e.g., cassava disease, soil management, pest control).
- Early exploration of empirical AI safety testing.

### 2. Crop Disease Safety Demo (in progress)
- Basic model for plant disease detection with added safety guardrails.
- Tests robustness against adversarial inputs or distributional shift (real farm conditions vs. clean data).
- Highlights how misalignment in ag-AI could lead to food security failures.
